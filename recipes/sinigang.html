Sinigang na Bangus Recipe

What is Sinigang na Bangus?

Sinigang na Bangus is a classic Filipino sour soup featuring tender milkfish (bangus) as the main ingredient. This comforting dish is known as its tangy and savory broth, which is traditionally flavored with tamarind and enhanced by addition of fresh vegetables like kangkong (water spinach), sitaw (string beans), and eggplant.

The natural flavor of the fish perfectly complements the sharpness of the sour broth, making making it a refreshing and hearty meal that's perfect for any occasion, especially during cooler weather. Sinigang na Bangus is both a nutritious and flavorful representation of Filipino home-cooked cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 kilo milkfish (cleaned and sliced)
  • 4 pcs okras (sliced)
  • 1 bundle string beans (cut in 2-inch length)
  • 1 bundle water spinach (bite-size cut)
  • 1 pc radish (sliced)
  • 1 pc red onion (quartered)
  • 2 pcs tomatoes (quartered)
  • 1 pack Knorr tamarind powder
  • 3 cups of water
  • Fish sauce
  • Ground black pepper

Cook

  1. First, boil 3 cups of water in a cooking pot, then add the sliced radish, red onion, and tomatoes. Let this cook for 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add the sliced milkfish. Also put the Knorr tamarind powder. Let simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Add the strings beans and okras. Stir. Cover and cook for 5 mins.
  4. Add fish sauce and ground black pepper.
  5. Lastly, add the water spinach. Cover the pot and turn off the heat. Let is stay for 7 mins.
  6. Put it to a serving bowl. Serve.
